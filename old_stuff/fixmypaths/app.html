The FixMyPaths App

The FixMyPaths Android app allows you to report the problems on rights of way while out on a walk. It currently works in Hampshire, West Sussex, Wiltshire and Surrey. Say you come across a missing signpost, or a blocked path, you can fire up the app and report the problem directly to the FixMyPaths database. If you are in Hampshire, the problems will be sent directly to Hampshire County Council (it is the intention to expand out of Hampshire in the future). The app will contact the FixMyPaths server to find out if there is a nearby right of way, and if there is, you will be given the chance to enter the problem report. The report will be stored in FixMyPaths' own database and emailed to Hampshire County Council if in Hampshire. The app is in an early stage of development and has been tested by myself live in-the-field, but cannot be guaranteed to be bug-free. Nonetheless bug reports are welcomed. So if you live in or near this part of southern England, like walking, and have an Android 2.1+ phone, please download the app and get testing!

IMPORTANT! Please note that the ACRA library has been included in FixMyPaths. This will send off a bug report if the app crashes for any reason, with the possible effect (as is the case for any data sent to a server) of incurring charges to your phone account. This is to help debugging. Please do not install if you are not happy with this!

Instructions to use the app

Disclaimer: Installing the FixMyPaths app to your phone is at your own risk. It is software at an early stage of development; it appears to be stable in tests but please do not blame me if it blows up your phone. With that warning over, here are the instructions on how to install and use the app.

  1. First of all configure your phone to allow installation of non-Market (Google Play) apps. This will be under Settings: on a Samsung Galaxy SIII, it is under "Security and Lock Screen". On the HTC Hero, it's under "Applications". So try a settings category that looks like either of those two.
  2. When the app is run for the first time, you will be prompted to download a small style file: a file which specifies the style of the maps. Download this: it's only 4K.
  3. You now need to install one or more maps. Simply run the app within a wifi area and select the "Download county map" option. Select one of the counties to download the map to the device. These maps are 4 to 9 megabytes so the download is best done in a wifi area.
    • If you do not have access to wifi, you might need to download the map file from a desktop or laptop and copy it to the phone's SD card. Connect your phone up to your desktop or laptop and find the SD card folder (on the Samsung Galaxy SIII you should select the Phone folder instead, as on this model the map is stored on the actual phone). Inside this folder, there is a folder called "openhants". Download the appropriate map file from here and place it in the "openhants" folder. If you are having difficulty with this step please email me on freemapinfo at gmail dot com.
  4. Having downloaded one or more maps, choose the current map with the "Choose county map" option.
  5. You should now be ready to go. Ensure that GPS is switched on, fill in your name and email in the preferences (menu-preferences), find somewhere suitable and report your problem! (these must be real problems if in Hampshire, otherwise the council will get it!) There will soon be a "Download existing problems" option which ensures that you do not try to report an existing problem by showing them on the map.
  6. As well as reporting a problem at the precise location it occurs, you can also report a problem by tapping the map at an arbitrary location (as long as it's near a path, obviously!) Through the preference options, you can also specify to save problems on the phone while out walking (rather than uploading to the server) and then upload when you get back.
  7. Please report your experiences to me, Nick Whitelegg at freemapinfo at gmail dot com.

System requirements

Android 2.1+ needed. I have tested it on two devices: on the one hand an HTC Hero and on the other a Samsung Galaxy SIII. On the Hero the map drawing is rather slow and prone to occasional crashes while on the SIII it runs smoothly. I'd be interested to know what performance is like on different "in-between" models, again please email me (freemapinfo at gmail dot com).
